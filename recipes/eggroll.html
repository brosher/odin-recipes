Description: Hmong Eggrolls

Hmong eggrolls are fun to eat any time of year! Here is a very basic recipe. Experiment with other ingredients and add whatever you like to suit your taste!

Fun Fact: There are different and types of eggrolls that can be found in Chinese, Thai, Vietnamese, Hmong, and other East Asian restaurants. Each variations have slightly different ingredients, sizes, and thickness.

Ingredients

  • 2 lb ground pork
  • 2 cup shredded cabbage
  • 2 cup shredded carrots
  • 2 cup chopped onion
  • 2 Tbsp oyster sauce
  • 2 Tbsp salt
  • 1 package rice vermicelli cellophane noodles
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 pack (50 sheets) eggroll wrappers

Steps

  1. Add hot water to rice vermicelli cellophane noodles and let soak 5-10 minutes until soft.
  2. Take scissors and cut up the noodles.
  3. Mix meat, cabbage, carrots, onion, oyster sauce, salt, noodles, eggs until well combined.
  4. Peel the eggroll skins.
  5. Place eggroll skin on a flat surface. Add the eggroll mixture and roll up. Seal the ends with egg yolk.
  6. Fry in hot oil until golden brown.
